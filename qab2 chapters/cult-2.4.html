Shuttai 4h
People would find things to discriminate about even without quirks. Think about skin color, ethnicity, sex, height, IQ. I don't mean to speak directly against Prelude's personal ideals, but IMO removing people's quirks will in no way make the world a better place. All you're doing is trying to take away something that most people identify, consciously or unconsciously, as an integral part of themselves.

I've had the opportunity to personally interview people who have become unable to use their due to various circumstances. The majority of them are listless and have difficulty concentrating, as well as difficulty holding down a job. They often develop depression and some fall into suicidal idealization. This INCLUDES people who were previously documented as saying their quirk didn't really matter to them, and didn't use their quirks frequently. Maybe some of this is purely psychological. Maybe it isn't. The majority of these people had experienced traumatic injuries, and that could be a factor. But when compared with individuals with similar injuries, those who had lost the use of their quirk suffered from significantly greater alterations in cognition and mood.

I'm also not saying people without quirks are useless; I'm saying that quirks are embedded in DNA, and removing the quirk from a person could have INCREDIBLY unknown and unintended side effects, and that this is a much more complicated issue than people have recently been making it out to be.
