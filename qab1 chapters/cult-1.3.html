Answer: (tl;dr: QAB posted an article that Endeavor took offence to and issued a lawsuit. The internet (ie., Twitter) has proceeded to take sides flip its s#!t.)

QAB is short for "Quirk Analysis Blog for the Future", the long-winded title of a personal blog hosted by it's sole writer, "Shuttai".

QAB has been around for a few years, but started seeing popularity when few verified hero accounts on Twitter started publicly debating the content. Around the same time, QAB started to change the way it presented content; it's now publishing current event articles and has also started up a "quirk consultation" series. This seems to have done two things: made QAB controversial, and cement a die hard fanbase. (As an aside, the quirk consultations are incredibly well done and worth a read, especially for anyone interested in pursuing a career in quirk analysis. The fanbase votes on a list of submitted quirks, and then QAB posts detailed dissertations on the most popular ones. He's an amateur (as in, not classically trained/no accreditation), but the series has been garnering a lot of attention.)

Earlier this week QAB posted a more political piece about media coverage in heroics. It talks about how heroics is turning into a "popularity contest", and turning some heros into "dangerously unstable megalomaniacs [...] like Endeavor." Endeavor's agency very quickly tried to sue on the basis of defamation or libel, but 1, suing an anonymous internet user is no joke, 2, the post isn't against the terms of service for the service provider he uses, and 3, as far as anyone can tell, Endeavor wasn't actually slandered. (Not by the legal definition, anyway.)

There's rumors that a SECOND lawsuit was filed against QAB for how the fanbase reacted to the first lawsuit, but I haven't seen any solid proof of that.

Now it's a full-blown Twitter war. Even some of my IRL coworkers have strong opinions about what is ultimately one anonymous guy on the internet publicly publishing his political views... just like everyone on Twitter is doing already. Someone with more of a sociology background might be able to tell you why the article has been so polarizing.

Concerning the Lawsuit
by Shuttai on July 05, 20X4

 

Hey everyone! I apologize that this isn't the quirk analysis you were probably expecting. Instead, I wanted to address the recent rumors and drama.

 

Did Endeavor sue you?
Endeavor's publicity team tried to threaten me with a lawsuit. This was done in an effort to intimidate me into removing an article I posted early last week. They believed the article did not "frame Endeavor in an accurate or publicly respectable manner", which I think was their way of accusing me of defamation. (If you're reading this, you've probably read the offending post already. If you haven't, here it is.) It is my suspicion that Endeavor's publicity team did not, in fact, read the article they tried to sue me over, as the lines they took issue with were taken out of context. The lawsuit threat (threat) has subsequently been dropped.

 

Do you have a personal grudge against Endeavor?
Endeavor has done a lot for Japan, and many grateful people owe him their lives. Again, if you've read the original post, I think you'll see that my criticisms are against the current structure of the hero industry; NOT against Endeavor.

 

Regarding Hawks
Hawks and I are not fighting, and we don't hate each other. I don't even know Hawks! To be honest, I'd be embarrassed to try to talk to him even over the internet. He's so famous?? He's?? So cool?? I'm pretty sure the one time he allegedly read my blog was just a fluke or something, I'm sure he's too busy for the ramblings of an amature quirk enthusiast. In the off chance his recent tweet WAS in response to this blog, then I am honored he read my post and I respect his opinion! He's given me a lot to think about. My perspective is that from the outside looking in, and having to live in a world where every decision the Hero Public Safety Commission makes can affect my daily life and well-being, not that of a professional in the hero industry. And on the topic of other's opinions, please keep in mind that everyone is entitled to their own! We're all walking through life using a different lens of upbringing and experience. We all have different opinions and ideas on what we think is the right way to change the world for the better; and I think in the end, the melding of those ideas is what will ultimately give us the power to remake the world the way we want to see it.
